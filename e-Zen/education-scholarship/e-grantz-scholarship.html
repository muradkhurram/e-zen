What is this?

The E-Grantz Scholarship architecture is an integrated centralized system that provides post-matric educational assistance to students from marginalized communities. Operating through real-time verification modules, it ensures direct bank disbursal of tuition fees, non-refundable examination costs, boarding allowances, and pocket stipends for degree, diploma, and technical courses.

Core Program Eligibility

  • SC / ST / OEC Categories: Eligible for complete concession benefits and monthly stipends with no annual income cap constraints.
  • OBC & General-EWS Categories: Eligible for specified fee concessions subject to strict state income thresholds (generally under 本地 parameter rows e.g., ₹1 Lakh for regular degree sets).
  • Admission Route: Must have secured entry via approved merit lists or general government allocation streams (seats inside self-financing college management blocks are strictly evaluated).

Required Validation Folder (Checklist)

💡 Tap target checkmarks to verify folder configuration parameters:

  • Verified Caste & Domicile Document Digital revenue certificate validating social category and community row.
  • Current Income Sheet Required for OBC/EWS streams to establish income limits. Must be valid for the ongoing financial period.
  • Allotment Memo & College Fee Balance Slip Document confirming institution admission date, allocation row, and verified fee criteria.
  • Aadhaar Link Verification String NPCI mapping confirmation for the candidate's core savings account.

Application & Processing Flow

System Status: Active 2026 Academic Session Sync Verification Channel: Institute INO ➔ Directorate Desk Clear
Phase 1: One-Time Profile Sync
  1. Complete your unified registration using your Aadhaar credentials (see Student Registration page).
  2. Log in to the E-Grantz module and map your current institution code along with your exact course admission track.
Phase 2: College Office Verification
  1. The campus clerk or Institute Nodal Officer (INO) checks your allotment logs against your digital uploads.
  2. The college signs off on attendance records and pushes the fee structure upward to the welfare board.
Phase 3: Sanction and Fee Release
  1. The District Welfare Directorate cross-checks the institutional flags and issues a formal sanction number.
  2. Tuition elements route to the college bank account, while personal monthly stipends route to the student’s ledger.

⚠️ Common Rejection Triggers & Blind Spots

  • NPCI Seeding Dropouts: The transaction fails at the state ledger stage because the student's bank account is not properly mapped on the Aadhaar mapper hub.
  • Course Re-Admission Missteps: Changing courses midway or moving to a different college without filing an official transfer update on the E-Grantz portal, halting subsequent releases.
  • Incorrect Fee Category Input: Selecting a "Free Seat" label instead of a "Payment/Merit" descriptor, creating an itemization mismatch inside the college validation queue.

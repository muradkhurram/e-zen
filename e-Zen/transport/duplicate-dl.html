What is this?

A Duplicate Driving Licence is an official legal replacement card generated when a driver's original licence token is lost, stolen, severely mutilated, or completely destroyed. Issued directly from the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways central repository, it preserves your existing license tracking numbers, vehicle class authorizations, and original expiry timelines.

Why is it needed?

Carrying a valid driving credential is a strict mandate when operating physical motor vehicles in public areas. If your original card becomes missing or illegible, securing a duplicate is necessary to avoid traffic enforcement fines and to protect your standing with vehicular insurance providers during claim evaluations.

Eligibility

  • Status Condition: The original licence must have been active, valid, and not currently suspended, revoked, or impounded by any judicial authority or enforcement bureau.
  • Jurisdiction Rule: Applications should ideally be processed through the specific RTO that originally issued the card, or the RTO holding authority over your current permanent residence.

Required Documents (Checklist)

💡 Tap the boxes to track what parameters you have structured for upload:

  • Police First Information Report (FIR) / Lost Property Certificate Mandatory for stolen or lost cases. A copy of the online missing complaint report filed with local police station.
  • Form LLD (Application for Duplicate DL) The official declaration sheet explaining the circumstances of loss, automatically compiled during the online filing phase.
  • Physical Damaged Licence Card (If Applicable) Required only if applying due to structural mutilation, defacement, or broken plastic smart cards.
  • Identity and Address Verification Proofs Clean scans of your Aadhaar Card, Passport, or Voter ID to anchor user parameters.

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: June 2026 Source: Parivahan MoRTH System Desk
Before You Begin
  • 🔒 Aadhaar Authentication: Choosing the "Aadhaar Authentication" track allows for completely contactless electronic duplicate processing in many states, removing the requirement to visit the physical RTO window.
Phase 1: Digital Index Query
  1. Click Open Sarathi Parivahan Portal above, select your home State, and choose **Services on DL**.
  2. Input your exact alphanumeric Driving Licence number string and date of birth. Click **Get DL Details**.
  3. Verify your recorded name, picture image, and category permissions on the screen interface.
  4. Check the specific transaction box labeled **Issue of Duplicate DL** and confirm.
Phase 2: Document Attestation Upload
  1. Complete the Form LLD questionnaire panels detailing why and where the original card was lost or broken.
  2. Upload your police missing report copy or scan your damaged physical card.
  3. Attach clear digital file representations of your Aadhaar card identity metadata proofs.
  4. Upload a crisp signature scan file and passport photo image matching standard dimension bounds.
Phase 3: Checkout and Post Dispatch
  1. Navigate to the integrated payment console and pay the statutory duplicate processing fees.
  2. Print the generated application acknowledgment summary receipt layout for tracking purposes.
  3. For contactless tracking, the system routes the application straight to the inspector desk. For traditional tracks, drop off physical document forms at the RTO counter.
  4. The newly generated replacement smart card is dispatched directly to your registered address via post.

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Illegible Police Document Files: Uploading blurry camera photos of the FIR log sheet where the station stamp or registration numbers cannot be read.
  • Unresolved Traffic Enforcement Holds: Active penal fines or court-ordered vehicle suspensions running against your licence index will lock all duplicate actions.
  • Data Form Spelling Disputes: Name arrangement structural changes typed on the duplicate web interface that conflict with baseline data records.

🔧 Troubleshooting

What should you do if your original legacy driving licence record was never digitized and doesn't show up in the Parivahan database?
You cannot directly apply for an online duplicate. You must first initiate a physical "Backlog Entry" workflow. Visit the original issuing RTO with old paper copy proofs or your licence booklet, submit a ledger verification request, and wait for the staff to manually input your historical file into the national electronic database. Once digitized, the online duplicate pipeline will open immediately.

FAQ

Q: Will the newly printed duplicate licence feature an extended validity window?
A: No. A duplicate card strictly mirrors the original data parameters. The expiration dates and class constraints remain identical to your old card.


Q: Is an online digital police complaint slip accepted in place of a standard physical station FIR?
A: Yes. Modern state police portal digital download receipts for lost articles are legally valid and accepted for upload on the Parivahan portal.

What is this?

A New Commercial Vehicle Permit is a statutory authorization framework issued under the Motor Vehicles Act. It legally empowers a registered motor vehicle to operate for commercial transit, hauling commercial freight, or carrying commercial passengers across specified regional boundaries or nationwide transport corridors.

Why is it needed?

Even if a commercial vehicle possesses a valid Registration Certificate (RC), it is strictly prohibited from operating for hire, reward, or logistics business use without a valid permit matching its transit layout. Operating without it constitutes a severe statutory infraction, resulting in high enforcement fines, vehicle impoundment at transport checkposts, and the complete cancellation of commercial insurance cover notes.

Key Permit Structures

  • National Permit (NP): Granted to goods carriages to transit seamlessly across four or more states nationwide under an integrated composite fee system.
  • State/Goods Carriage Permit: Restricts commercial logistics distribution strictly to paths within a specific home state boundary.
  • Contract Carriage Permit: Issued to passenger vehicles (taxis, tourist buses) to ferry passengers along a dedicated route or contractual layout.

Required Application Folder (Checklist)

💡 Tap the boxes to track your verification paperwork updates:

  • Valid Vehicle Registration Certificate (RC) The underlying vehicle must be pre-registered as a commercial carrier in the database.
  • Unexpired Fitness Certificate (Form 38) Valid proof showing the asset has successfully cleared the automated mechanical testing track.
  • Insurance Policy Cover Note & PUCC Active commercial insurance configuration alongside an unexpired emissions ticket.
  • Local Tax Clearance Logs Receipt entries confirming that all state road taxes have been fully settled up to the current date.

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: June 2026 Source: Parivahan MoRTH System Blueprint
Phase 1: Profile Initialization & Selection
  1. Click **Open Vahan Permit Portal** above, type in your vehicle registration number, and select access.
  2. Verify your ownership parameters and choose **Apply for New Permit** from the navigation links.
  3. Input your vehicle chassis sequence validation strings to open the operational fields.
Phase 2: Operational Data Entry
  1. Select your target permit configuration layout (e.g., National Permit, Private Service Goods, Contract Carriage).
  2. Specify your intended transit routing bounds, regional operating circles, or target multi-state corridors.
  3. Upload your compiled document PDF bundles containing the active commercial RC, unexpired fitness token, and valid emissions testing log sheet.
Phase 3: Checkout Ledger Clearance
  1. Advance to the online e-treasury checkout layout to settle the initial application and authorization fees.
  2. Download and print your unique application reference layout receipt showing successful payment mapping.
  3. The application file routes to your local RTA board desk for formal administrative review.
  4. Once verified and approved, download the newly generated digital permit authorization sheet directly from the portal console.

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Age Limit Restrictions: Certain state transport boards enforce maximum vehicle age limits (e.g., vehicles older than 12 or 15 years) for granting new multi-state National Permits.
  • Active Enforcement Flags: Unpaid traffic camera challans or outstanding checkpost citations recorded against the vehicle will block the permit application process.
  • Mismatched Insurance Parameters: Uploading a standard private vehicle insurance policy instead of a dedicated commercial vehicle liability policy cover note.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Is your application status showing as "Pending Board Approval" for an extended period?
Do not submit a duplicate request. Certain categories, like State Stage Carriage or Tourist Passenger permits, require a formal periodic review by the regional transport authority committee. Check the online portal tracking screen to see if your application has been assigned a specific RTA board hearing date. If required, you can attend the local RTO board session with your physical documentation folder to secure an immediate manual sign-off.

FAQ

Q: What is the initial validity period of a newly issued National Permit?
A: A brand-new National Permit is issued for a fixed operational timeline of 5 years, provided the composite multi-state corridor levies are paid annually.


Q: Can I transfer a commercial permit to another person if I sell the vehicle asset?
A: Yes. You must file a joint application layout for **Permit Transfer** along with your vehicle ownership transfer paperwork to safely reassign the operating permit tracking row to the new owner.

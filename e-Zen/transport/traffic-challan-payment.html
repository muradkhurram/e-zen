What is this?

Digital Traffic Challan Settlement is the electronic legal workflow used to clear official traffic enforcement citations issued by highway interceptors, automated speed cameras, or traffic officers. Logged instantly against either a driver's licence string or a vehicle registration index, this system enables direct digital payment of dynamic penal tariffs.

Why is it needed?

Unresolved traffic challans quickly freeze your vehicle profile within the national database. The Vahan platform will completely block transactions like insurance renewals, vehicle transfers, address changes, or fitness assessments until your citation history is fully settled and cleared.

Eligibility

  • Payment Period: Citations remain eligible for direct online payment for up to 60 days from the issue date. After this window, unpaid cases are automatically forwarded to regional Virtual Courts for formal judicial action.
  • Contest Restrictions: Paying a fine online constitutes an admission of the traffic violation. If you wish to contest the citation or plead not guilty, do not pay online; instead, choose the option to route the file to a virtual or physical court hearing.

Required Information (Checklist)

💡 Tap the boxes to track what parameters you have gathered for verification:

  • Challan Alphanumeric Number The unique citation tracking sequence delivered to your registered mobile number via SMS or printed on a physical notice.
  • Vehicle Registration Number & Chassis/Engine Strings Your standard plate sequence accompanied by the final 5 characters of either your engine or chassis stamp.
  • Driving Licence (DL) Alphanumeric Sequence Required if the citation was issued directly to the driver's credential rather than the vehicle asset profile.

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: June 2026 Source: National E-Challan Core Operations
Before You Begin
  • 📸 Review Photographic Evidence: Always inspect the automated camera image captures or location data attached to your e-challan record to ensure your vehicle plate was not misread by the optical tracking systems before finalizing payment.
Phase 1: Citation Lookup & Authentication
  1. Click **Open MoRTH E-Challan Portal** above and select **Check Challan Status**.
  2. Input your unique **Challan Number**, **Vehicle Number**, or **DL Number** string to query the database.
  3. Complete the security verification and click **Get Detail** to view your active violation history rows.
  4. Review the detailed row metrics: infraction type, timestamp, location, and the attached photographic proof.
Phase 2: Payment Execution
  1. Locate your specific pending citation rows and click the **Pay Now** button layout item.
  2. Verify your linked mobile number by entering the system-generated One Time Password (OTP).
  3. The system will automatically redirect you to your respective state's official digital e-treasury payment gateway (such as GRAS, eGRAS, or IFMS).
  4. Select your preferred secure payment method (Netbanking, UPI, or Credit/Debit Card) and complete the transaction.
Phase 3: Receipt Validation & Clearance
  1. Once payment is successful, download and save the official printed payment receipt containing your unique transaction tracking numbers.
  2. Verify that your challan status switches from **Pending** to **Disposed** within the Parivahan database framework.
  3. The Vahan administrative engine will immediately clear the hold on your vehicle record, allowing you to resume normal administrative tasks.

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Gateway Transaction Timeouts: Network communication drops during the e-treasury checkout loop can stall payments, leaving the challan active while funds are temporarily held by the bank.
  • Court Relocation Status: If a citation's 60-day payment window expires, the portal will remove the online payment button and mark the status as "Sent to Court," blocking direct digital settlement.
  • Compounding Restrictions: Certain major traffic violations cannot be settled via simple portal payments and require a mandatory physical appearance before an RTO officer or a magistrate.

🔧 Troubleshooting

What should you do if your payment was successfully deducted from your bank account, but the e-challan status still shows as "Pending"?
Do not attempt to repay the fine. Transaction synchronization on state treasury networks can occasionally experience brief processing lags. Log back into the portal, navigate to your history summary, and click the **Check Payment Verification** button next to the stalled row. The engine will perform a manual reconciliation with the bank ledger and instantly update your status to "Disposed."

FAQ

Q: My citation status is marked as "Sent to Virtual Court." How can I clear it?
A: You can no longer pay through the standard e-challan portal. Instead, visit the official **Virtual Courts Portal (vcourts.gov.in)**, select your state, search by your vehicle number, and pay the judicial fine online to clear the case file.


Q: Can I check or pay for traffic violations issued outside my home state?
A: Yes. The MoRTH electronic system consolidates traffic citations nationwide across participating states, allowing you to view and settle cross-border infractions seamlessly.

What is this?

This service provides an immediate channels tracking interface for lodging grievances regarding power delivery line drops, line snaps, billing tracking adjustments, voltage fluctuations, transformer sparks, or localized property meter hardware bugs.

Why is it needed?

Filing issues through official digital routes ensures your ticket maps directly to the live duty tracking system of the respective local Breakdown Crew. It assigns an auditable reference code token, tracks time-to-resolve performance, and routes high-priority hazard alerts to safety supervisors instantly.

Required Details (Checklist)

💡 Tap the boxes to track what you have gathered:

  • 13-Digit Consumer Number Required for individual premises power cuts or billing issue tickets
  • Contact Mobile Number Used to receive status update alerts and connect with the field crew
  • Location Landmark Reference Crucial for community complaints like loose wires or damaged street posts

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: 21 June 2026 Source: KSEB Customer Relations Cell
Before You Begin
  • Check Surrounding Area: Verify if the power outage affects your entire block or just your individual property breaker switch.
  • Hazard Emergency Rule: For severe emergencies like snapped live wires or fires, skip online forms and call 1912 immediately.
Phase 1: Ticket Logging Paths
  1. Open the KSEB Quick Complaint Web panel or boot up the official 'KSEB' mobile app interface.
  2. Input your 13-digit Consumer Number (or select "General Category / Public Hazard Search" to bypass connection requirements).
  3. Verify your basic registration metadata profiles displayed on screen layers.
Phase 2: Categorization & Description
  1. Choose your issue type from the menu category filters (e.g., No Power, Voltage Issues, Meter Fault, Safety Risk).
  2. Add specific descriptions detailing landmarks or particular issue traits (e.g., "transformer smoking after storm").
  3. Provide your active contact mobile number to handle ground communication requirements.
Phase 3: Dispatch & Reference Tracking
  1. Click the 'Submit Complaint' action button to route your ticket down to the active local station dashboard.
  2. Save the automatically generated digital confirmation reference ID token sent via SMS text.
  3. Use the dashboard tracker profile sheet link later to check resolution logs or follow up on your ticket.

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Internal ELCB Tripping Issues: Outage issues caused by faults within the consumer's private wiring rather than a grid failure.
  • Wrong Section Mapping Logs: Filing community public complaints under inaccurate village/section zones, routing the crew to the wrong area.
  • Vague Location Guidelines: Leaving out landmark details for line-snaps, causing repair crews to miss the targeted site.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Complaint logged but status reads "Resolved" while power is still out? This typically means the line team fixed the main neighborhood transformer block, but your individual phase fuse might still be blown. Log a follow-up "Individual Phase Outage" ticket.


Web portal fails during heavy storm events? High-density weather events can overload regional network servers. Use the automated interactive voice response system by dialing 1912 as an immediate backup route.

FAQ

Q: Are there service fees for registering electrical maintenance complaints?
A: No. All standard breakdown repairs, fuse replacements, and safety line clearances are performed free of charge by KSEB field crews.


Q: How do I track tickets filed via the 1912 phone helpline?
A: Phone queries generate the exact same digital tracking tokens. The reference number is texted to your phone for easy tracking on the web portal.

What is this?

This structural tracking system registers citizen files to secure a fresh, direct public utility pipe water supply branch connection into domestic, institutional, or commercial properties from adjacent distribution mains.

Why is it needed?

Securing a certified water supply connection requires formal department screening to confirm pipeline network pressure, verify layout ownership rights, calculate connection distances, and arrange valid municipal road cutting permits.

Required Documents (Checklist)

💡 Tap the boxes to track what you have gathered:

  • Proof of Land Ownership Latest Land Tax Receipt or clear Possession Certificate copy
  • Building Residential Card / Number Slip Issued by the relevant Local Self Government (Panchayat/Municipality/Corporation)
  • Identity Verification File Aadhaar Card, Electoral Voters Card, or active Passport copies
  • Licensed Plumber Scheme Map A site outline drawn and certified by an authorized KWA licensed plumbing professional

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: 21 June 2026 Source: KWA Distribution Allocation Code
Before You Begin
  • Hire a Licensed Plumber: All structural drawings and work pipelines must be executed through a registered, certified KWA professional.
  • Road Authority Check: If the connection line needs to cross public tarmac surfaces, be ready for supplementary PWD or Local Body clearance fees.
Phase 1: Registration Entry
  1. Access the official consumer services interface page on the main KWA web portal.
  2. Create a user login profile using your communication phone number and current credentials.
  3. Select the "Apply for New Water Connection" action link to start your application form.
Phase 2: Filing Metadata & Uploads
  1. Select your matching geographic region parameters (District, Local Body, and closest KWA Section Office).
  2. Fill out connection fields like property category, pipe diameter in millimeters, and distance from distribution mains.
  3. Upload your compiled PDF documents, land verification files, and the licensed plumber's plan view.
Phase 3: Inspection & Meter Hook-up
  1. A local water authority field inspector conducts a site visit to verify plumbing lines and distance metrics.
  2. Pay the formal balance estimates generated after inspection approval via your portal ledger desk link.
  3. The physical service pipe connection is drawn, a verified water meter device is fitted, and supply line services are activated.

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Unresolved Arrears on Plot: Rejections if the land layout carries outstanding historical utility dues.
  • Unlicensed Design Work: Submitting plumbing schema designs drafted by technicians without a valid KWA license badge.
  • Missing Local Body Documents: Failure to provide a valid building number or ownership structure proof.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Application status stuck on "Awaiting Permit Clearance"? If your line route crosses a public road, processing times depend on local authority road-cutting approvals. Use your tracking reference number to follow up at your local section desk.


Mismatched distance calculations showing? If the official inspector logs layout lengths that differ from your initial submission, the system automatically recalculates your material estimates before final payment checkout.

FAQ

Q: Can tenants apply for a fresh property water meter connection?
A: Yes. Tenants can apply by submitting a valid lease deed alongside an official No Objection Certificate (NOC) signed by the property owner.


Q: Who is responsible for sourcing the physical water meter hardware?
A: You can choose to buy an approved, company-tested meter device model independently or select options to rent one directly from KWA stocks.

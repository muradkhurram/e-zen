What is this?

The Ration Card is the essential household document issued by the Civil Supplies Department. It is required to purchase subsidized food grains through the Public Distribution System (PDS) and serves as a verified proof of residence and household structure for various welfare benefits.

Why is it needed?

It is the primary instrument for food security, enabling households to access essential commodities at subsidized rates. Beyond food, it serves as a crucial legal document for identity proof, address verification for other government services, and is mandatory for claiming various state-sponsored social welfare pensions and educational grants.

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: 21 June 2026 Source: Civil Supplies Dept. Circular
Before You Begin
  • Ensure Aadhaar-linked mobile: All members must have their Aadhaar updated with a mobile number to receive OTPs.
  • Keep scans ready: Have clear digital copies of all required documents to avoid upload failures.
Phase 1: Digital Enrollment
  1. Visit the official Civil Supplies e-portal.
  2. Register an account using your mobile number and Aadhaar authentication.
  3. Select "New Application" for a Ration Card.
Phase 2: Data Entry & Upload
  1. Enter details of all family members exactly as they appear on their Aadhaar cards.
  2. Upload all required scanned documents (Address proof, Income certificate).
  3. Submit the application and note down your Application Reference Number.
Phase 3: Verification & Issuance
  1. The application is sent to the local Taluk Supply Officer (TSO) for field inquiry.
  2. Once verified, your household is categorized (APL/BPL) and approved.
  3. Download the E-Ration Card directly from the portal once status reflects 'Approved'.

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Duplicate Entries: Name already exists on an active card in another jurisdiction.
  • Incorrect Data: Mismatched names or dates of birth compared to Aadhaar records.
  • Invalid Proofs: Address proof is older than the mandated duration or belongs to a different ward.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Application stuck at "Pending"? If no update is received within 30 days, visit your local Taluk Supply Office with your reference number for a manual status check.


Payment failures? If you encounter errors during fee payments, do not re-apply. Check the "View Status" menu to verify if the payment was recorded or if a refund is pending.

FAQ

Q: Can I apply for a new card if I moved districts?
A: You must first apply for a 'Deletion Certificate' from your old district/card before submitting a new application.


Q: What is the difference between APL and BPL?
A: BPL (Below Poverty Line) households are eligible for subsidized food grains and priority welfare support, whereas APL (Above Poverty Line) cards are primarily for identification and basic subsidy access.

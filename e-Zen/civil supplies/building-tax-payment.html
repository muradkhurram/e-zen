What is this?

This statutory administrative framework handles the One-Time Building Tax and annual Luxury Tax mandated under the Kerala Building Tax Act, 1975. This tax is distinct from regular property tax and is collected directly by state revenue officials based at local Village Offices.

Why is it needed?

Every newly constructed building exceeding specific plinth area limits must settle a one-time structural tax assessment. Residential structures with a plinth area exceeding 278.7 square meters (approx. 3000 sq. ft.) face an additional annual Luxury Tax. Clearing these assessments avoids formal revenue collection drops or legal blockades on your land registry certificates.

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: 21 June 2026 Source: Directorate of Land Revenue Manuals
Before You Begin
  • Assessment First: You cannot pay this tax online until your local Village Officer verifies your building measurements and the Taluk Office updates your file to show active dues.
  • Luxury Threshold: Track your square footage limits carefully if expanding your layout to avoid unexpected annual Luxury Tax charges.
Phase 1: Portal Profiling
  1. Navigate to the official Kerala Revenue e-Services portal window link.
  2. Log into your existing tracking account or create a consumer login profile using your Aadhaar number.
  3. Click on the main services panel and choose "Building Tax Payment".
Phase 2: Filing Assessment Reference
  1. Select your matching property parameters (District, Taluk, and specific local Village Office).
  2. Enter the Assessment Order Number or the digital Transaction Reference ID provided by the revenue department.
  3. Click "Fetch Details" to populate fields showing the owner's name, calculated plinth square footage, and due revenue tax balances.
Phase 3: Checkout Processing
  1. Verify that the balance on your screen layer matches your official paper assessment notices.
  2. Click "Proceed to Pay" and select your preferred secure e-Treasury transaction channel tool.
  3. Complete your online authorization check-out and save your digital e-Challan receipt safely.

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Unposted Assessment Profiles: Attempting to query connections online before your paper files are approved at the Taluk desk.
  • Wrong Village Selection: Choosing a geographically adjacent village office, which blocks the system from pulling your property data rows.
  • Expired Transaction Sessions: Taking too long on payment page verification steps, which causes secure e-Treasury portals to disconnect.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Your structural file is not loading on the online system? If your house was completed recently, the paper data may still be in transit. Bring your structural blueprints and local body certificates to your local Village Office to request a data entry update.


Challan status shows failed but money left your bank account? Do not attempt to pay a second time. Wait for internal reconciliation balances to update your portal profile automatically, which normally takes up to 4 days.

FAQ

Q: How does the Revenue Department calculate the One-Time Building Tax?
A: Calculations are structured around total plinth area square footage according to sliding scales detailed in the Kerala Building Tax Act schedules. Commercial layouts carry higher tax rates than residential structures.


Q: Does my annual property tax replace this state government building tax?
A: No. Sanchaya property tax is paid to your local Panchayat or Municipality to support civic services. This building tax goes directly to the state Treasury as a structural capital levy.

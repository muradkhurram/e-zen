What is this?

A One and Same Certificate (also known as an Identity Certificate or Name Variation Resolution sheet) is an official revenue document that clarifies identity errors. It formally certifies that multiple name variations, spelling errors, or initials across different documents belong to the single same individual. This certificate is required to resolve name variations between school books and identity cards during competitive university admission checkouts (**CLAT / CAP** pipelines), process immigration visas, or clear financial account settlement anomalies.

Core Verification Framework Rules

  • The Identity Link Baseline: To confirm that the documents refer to the same individual, anchor details such as birthdates, parental names, and structural signature styles must match across all files.
  • Non-Fraud Validation: This service is strictly used to resolve genuine clerical or spelling errors. It cannot be used to execute a formal, intentional name change, which requires a statutory deed poll and a formal gazette notification process.
  • Localized Record Audit: The local village office will verify the applicant's identity by cross-referencing regional ration ledgers, electoral rolls, and municipal registries.

Required Identity Verification Folder (Checklist)

💡 Check off items to audit your identity resolution bundle:

  • The Conflicting Primary Documents Clear scans of both documents showing the name variations (e.g., SSLC page vs. Passport).
  • Notarized Identity Affidavit A formal sworn statement signed before a Notary Public detailing the exact name variations.
  • Aadhaar Card and Electoral Roll Extracts Used to verify consistent address details and parental name strings.
  • Certificate of Local Ward Verification A formal declaration from your local municipal counselor confirming your identity within the community.

Application & Verification Pipeline

System Metric: Active 2026 Identity Alignment Database Processing Frame: Completed within 5–7 operational working days
Phase 1: Digital Registration & Variant Input
  1. Log into your regional e-District console and select the One and Same Certificate option link.
  2. Type each name variation exactly as it appears on your documents into the variant description boxes, then upload your support scans.
Phase 2: Local Revenue Field Inquiry
  1. The application routes to your local Village Officer, who cross-references your submitted documents with regional registry systems.
  2. The officer verifies that the name variations do not create an identity conflict with other registered individuals in the ward.
Phase 3: Secure Certificate Release
  1. Following local clearance, the Village Officer signs the certificate using a verified electronic signature.
  2. Download the finalized PDF document, which features an integrated cryptographic QR tracking code for institutional verification.

⚠️ Critical Processing Obstacles & Failures

  • Complete Structural Variations: Attempting to link entirely different names without providing secondary supporting records that confirm matching parental data.
  • Illegible Digital Uploads: Submitting low-resolution document scans where institutional stamps or names are obscured or difficult to read.
  • Attempting a Formal Name Change: Using this application track to change an active identity profile, which instead requires a formal gazette notification process.

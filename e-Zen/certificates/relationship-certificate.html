What is this?

A Relationship Certificate is a formal, legally binding revenue document issued to confirm the exact familial relationship or kinship tracking status between living family members (such as confirming parent-child status or sibling links). Unlike a legal heirship certificate, which is used to process inheritance claims after a family member passes away, a relationship certificate is used to process dependency-based benefits for living individuals. This includes securing corporate or government medical insurance extensions, claiming dependency points on visa files, processing family welfare cards, or transferring student allowances.

Core Kinship Verification Rules

  • Living Member Constraint: This tracking track explicitly covers relationships between living individuals. If the primary anchor relative has passed away, you must apply for a **Legal Heirship Certificate** instead.
  • Multi-Registry Cross-Checking: Verification relies on cross-checking your details across multiple databases, including matching parents' names on birth cards, official secondary school sheets, and your family's smart ration card registry.
  • Mandatory Local Inquiries: The local village office will verify family links by cross-referencing records and performing brief interviews with local ward members or neighbors.

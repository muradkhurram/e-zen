What is this?

An Income Certificate is an official statement issued by the Revenue Department that certifies the combined annual income of a citizen's entire family from all known legal sources.

Why is it needed?

It is mandatory for obtaining fee concessions in educational institutions, securing state or central scholarships (like E-Grantz), securing social welfare pensions, and validating eligibility for medical aid or housing schemes.

Eligibility

  • Resident Status: Must be a permanent resident of Kerala.
  • Family Scope: Income calculations include the applicant, parents, dependent siblings, or spouse living in the same household unit.

Required Documents (Checklist)

💡 Tap the boxes to track what you have gathered:

  • Aadhaar Card Mandatory identification reference.
  • Ration Card Used to verify family member list and household category.
  • Income Proof Salary Certificate (for salaried employees) OR Income IT return statement OR a self-declaration statement for daily wage earners.
  • Land/Property Tax Receipt Required to ensure agricultural earnings match official holdings.
  • Scanned Document Constraints PDF format only, file size must be strictly under 100KB per document.

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: June 2026 Source: Kerala IT Mission Portal Directions
Before You Begin
  • Scan documents to PDF: Do not upload photos; documents must be scanned as clear PDFs under 100KB.
  • Check your Village: Ensure you select the exact local Revenue Village where your residential property sits.
Phase 1: Portal Account Registration
  1. Click Go to Official Portal above to open e-District Kerala.
  2. Click on Portal User Registration if you do not have an account.
  3. Provide your Name, Aadhaar number, Date of Birth, and choose a unique User ID and Password.
  4. Verify your account using the OTP sent to your Aadhaar-registered mobile phone. Log into the workspace.
Phase 2: Form Submission & Payment
  1. On your user dashboard, click on Apply for a Certificate.
  2. Select Revenue Department from the drop-down list and choose Income Certificate.
  3. Fill in the Applicant Profile. Select your District, Taluk, and specific Revenue Village accurately.
  4. Select the purpose of your certificate (e.g., Educational, General Purpose).
  5. Input your family details and claim your total annual income amount.
  6. Upload your scanned PDFs (Ration Card, Salary Certificate, Aadhaar) under the attachment prompts.
  7. Click **Submit** to view the integrated e-treasury screen. Pay the fee of ₹15 using UPI, Netbanking, or Debit card. Download your transaction slip.
Phase 3: Downloading Your Approved Certificate
  1. The application routes directly to your local Village Officer (VO) for verification.
  2. Once verified and digitally signed by the VO, you will receive an SMS confirmation.
  3. Log back into the e-District portal, go to **Transaction History**, and click **Download Certificate**.

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Incorrect Village Selection: Choosing a neighboring village or municipal limit instead of your exact Revenue Village layout causes field verification to fail.
  • Omission of Salary Details: Government or organized sector employees claiming "agriculture/coolie" income without attaching an official Form 16 or Salary Slip will be flagged and rejected.
  • Legibility/Format Issues: Uploading image files (JPG) converted crudely or blurry PDFs over 100KB which cannot be read by the verification desk.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Payment status shows "Pending" or funds left bank account without confirmation?
Do not submit a second payment immediately. Log out of e-District, wait approximately 45 minutes, log back into your user dashboard, access **Payment History**, and click **Refresh Status** to manually sync the e-Treasury server ledger.

FAQ

Q: What is the validity period of an Income Certificate in Kerala?
A: An Income Certificate issued via the e-District platform is officially valid for **one year** from its date of issuance.


Q: Can married individuals use their parent's income?
A: For general eligibility or employment, a married person's family unit consists of their spouse and dependent children rather than their parents.

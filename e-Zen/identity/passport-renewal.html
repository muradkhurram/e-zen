What is this?

Passport Renewal (officially termed "Re-Issue") is the process of generating a fresh passport booklet to replace an existing passport. It is required when the current passport is nearing its expiration date, has run out of blank visa pages, or has sustained physical damage.

Why is it needed?

Most foreign nations require a passport to have **at least 6 months of validity** remaining beyond your intended travel dates to grant entry or clear visa applications. Renewing your passport on time keeps your international status active and prevents sudden travel disruptions.

Eligibility

  • Timing: You can apply for a re-issue up to **1 year before** the passport's expiration date or at any point after it has expired.
  • Exhausted Pages: Eligible at any time if your current booklet has fewer than 2 blank pages remaining.
  • Profile Updates: Required if there is a change in core personal details, such as updating your surname post-marriage or changing your permanent home address record.

Required Documents (Checklist)

💡 Tap the boxes to track what you need to bring along with your old passport booklet:

  • Old Passport Booklet The original physical passport booklet along with self-attested photocopies of its first two and last two pages.
  • Proof of Current Address (If Address has Changed) Your updated Aadhaar Card, an active Bank Passbook with photo, or a utility bill matching your new residence. *(No proof is needed if your address remains the same as your old passport).*
  • Documentary Proof for Profile Changes (Optional) Official Marriage Certificate (for surname changes) or a government Gazette notification for structural legal name edits.

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: June 2026 Source: Passport Seva Portal Operations Manual
Before You Begin
  • 🔒 Check for Validity Hold: If your personal data and address remain unchanged from your old passport, your application may qualify for a "No Police Verification" track, significantly accelerating printing and delivery.
Phase 1: Online Portal Lodging & Payment
  1. Click Apply for Re-Issue above to access the secure Passport Seva system. Log in with your active account details.
  2. Click on Apply for Fresh Passport/Re-issue of Passport.
  3. Select Re-issue of Passport and specify the reason (e.g., Validity Expired/Due to Expire, Pages Exhausted, or Change in Personal Particulars).
  4. Fill out the form fields. Ensure your personal details exactly match your existing passport unless you are intentionally requesting a profile update.
  5. Submit the data form, pay the statutory fee of ₹1,500 online, and pick an appointment slot at your nearest Passport Seva Kendra (PSK).
Phase 2: Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) Processing
  1. Go to the PSK venue at your scheduled time. Bring your old physical passport booklet, photocopies, and any required address update proofs.
  2. At Counter A, the operator will take your new biometric fingerprint scans and digital headshot photo for the new booklet.
  3. At Counter B, the verification officer checks your old passport data and matches any new profile update proofs.
  4. At Counter C, the granting officer cancels your old passport booklet by stamping it **"Cancelled"** and handing it back to you. They will then approve the issuance of your new booklet.
Phase 3: Verification Check & Delivery Track
  1. If you have changed your permanent address, your file is routed to your local police station for a residency check. If your details have not changed, this verification step is often waived entirely.
  2. Once cleared, the high-security printing facility generates your new booklet, featuring a new unique passport number sequence.
  3. Your new booklet is sent directly to your residence via India Post Speed Post. *(Your old cancelled passport remains yours to keep, as it contains any active visas you may still hold).*

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Unreported Active Visas: Damaging or tearing pages containing active foreign visas. Always let the Counter C official handle your booklet carefully to preserve your active visas.
  • Address Discrepancies on Update: Requesting an address update but providing documents that do not completely match your new residential details.
  • Failing to Disclose Lost Booklets: If your passport is lost or stolen, you cannot apply via standard re-issue workflows. You must file a formal Lost Passport application along with an official Police FIR report.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Are you unsure whether your existing foreign visas remain valid after your old passport is cancelled?
Do not worry. The physical cancellation stamp placed on your old passport only invalidates the booklet itself for travel purposes. Any long-term tourist or work visas inside remain valid. When traveling abroad, simply carry both your old cancelled booklet and your new active passport together to clear border immigration checklines seamlessly.

FAQ

Q: Does my old passport number carry over to my newly renewed passport booklet?
A: No. Every renewed passport receives a brand-new alphanumeric passport number sequence. Your old passport number is permanently archived as your historical cross-reference index.


Q: Can I use the priority Tatkaal track for a re-issue if my passport was heavily damaged or washed in laundry?
A: No. Cases involving severely damaged, charred, or lost passports require a detailed administrative review. They cannot be processed under the fast-track Tatkaal system and must follow standard application routing instead.

What is this?

A Permanent Account Number (PAN) is a unique, 10-character alphanumeric identifier issued in the form of a laminated smart card by the Income Tax Department. It tracks financial workflows and prevents tax evasion by anchoring all financial transactions to a single citizen profile.

Why is it needed?

A PAN card is mandatory for core daily financial tasks. Common citizens require it to open any bank savings account, receive taxable salaries, apply for bank loans, buy or sell vehicles, invest in mutual funds, or receive payments exceeding ₹50,000.

Eligibility

  • All Individuals: Any citizen of India, including minors (applied through a parent or legal guardian acting as a representative assessee).
  • No Minimum Income Requirement: You do not need to be a taxpayer to apply for a PAN card; it is widely utilized purely as a standard national identity document.

Step-by-Step Procedure

Last Verified: June 2026 Source: Income Tax Department Digital Guidelines
Before You Begin
  • 📱 Check Aadhaar Mobile Link: To instantly generate your PAN without couriering physical papers, make sure your Aadhaar is linked to an active mobile number that can receive security OTP codes.
Method 1: Instant Paperless e-KYC (Fastest & Easiest)
  1. Click Apply via Official Portal above to open the secure Protean Online Services screen.
  2. Select New PAN - Indian Citizen (Form 49A) as the Application Type, and choose Individual from the category drop-down.
  3. Fill in your title, last name, first name, date of birth, email ID, and mobile number. Submit to generate a temporary Token Number sent to your email.
  4. Click "Continue with PAN Application Form" and select the option: Submit digitally through e-KYC & e-Sign (Paperless).
  5. Confirm if you require a physical laminated card delivered home (fees apply) or an electronic e-PAN only.
  6. Enter the last 4 digits of your Aadhaar card number and agree to let the portal use your Aadhaar photograph.
  7. Proceed straight to the payment screen. Pay ₹107 via UPI, Debit Card, or Netbanking.
  8. Authenticate via Aadhaar OTP validation. Once confirmed, your application signs digitally. A password-protected e-PAN link will drop into your email inbox within a few hours.
Method 2: Scanned Document Upload (To use a custom photo/signature)
  1. Follow Phase 1 above, but select the second option: Submit scanned images through e-Sign.
  2. Manually type out your Father's name, Mother's name, and select your specific income sourcing category block.
  3. Enter your local Assessing Officer (AO) code details. *(The portal provides a quick built-in lookup utility based on your state and city location limits)*.
  4. Upload a high-resolution color scan of your passport photograph, your physical signature signed in black ink, and your Aadhaar PDF file. Keep each file under 50KB.
  5. Pay the regulatory fee of ₹107 online and perform the final Aadhaar mobile OTP authentication step to finalize submission.

⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons

  • Signature Alignment Failures: Uploading blurry, low-contrast signature scans or signing inside the border using blue gel pens or pencil tracks. Always sign cleanly on blank white paper using dark black ink.
  • Data Mismatches against Aadhaar: If the spelling of your name or date of birth typed into the PAN application differs by even a single character from your Aadhaar record, the structural automated processing engine will throw a hard fault and reject it.
  • Unreadable Upload Files: Compressing document PDF files down too far, rendering identity details or photo profiles completely illegible to checking clerks.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Is your payment deducted but the portal crashed before displaying an acknowledgement or token slip code?
Do not start a fresh transaction from scratch. Check your registered email inbox for the initial temporary Token Number. Return to the Protean homepage login screen, click the **Registered User** tab, enter your token index, and click refresh. The system will recover your draft profile data and sync your payment status instantly.

FAQ

Q: What is the penalty for accidentally holding more than one PAN card profile?
A: Possessing more than one active PAN account number is illegal under Section 272B of the Income Tax Act, 1961, and can attract a strict statutory penalty of **₹10,000**. If you possess a duplicate card, surrender it immediately via the official correction forms.


Q: What is the open password key format to unpack the downloaded e-PAN PDF guide file?
A: The electronic file envelope is locked for privacy safety. The decryption security password key is your exact date of birth entered in the consecutive numeric layout layout: **DDMMYYYY** (e.g., if born on 05 May 1998, type 05051998).
